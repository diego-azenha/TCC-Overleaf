This work investigates factor investing from the perspective of probabilistic machine learning through the replication and application of the NeuralFactors model, proposed by Gopal (2024), to the Brazilian stock market. Unlike traditional multifactor models, in which factors are defined exogenously and estimated through linear regressions, the proposed approach explicitly models the joint distribution of returns and learns latent factors directly from data within a generative framework. The methodology enables the simultaneous estimation of dynamic factor exposures and the conditional covariance structure of assets, incorporating uncertainty explicitly into the modeling process. Model performance is evaluated using probabilistic metrics and portfolio optimization applications to assess whether endogenous factor learning delivers statistical and economic improvements relative to conventional approaches. The study thus offers a probabilistic reformulation of factor investing in the context of the Brazilian market.
